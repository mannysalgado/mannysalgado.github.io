What is Authoritative Parenting?

The Art of Correctly Parenting

From the website feedingthesoil

"The parent is demanding and responsive. Authoritative parenting is characterized by a child-centered approach that holds high expectations of maturity. Authoritative parents can understand how their children are feeling and teach them how to regulate their feelings. Even with high expectations of maturity, authoritative parents are usually forgiving of any possible shortcomings."

Why Authoritative Parenting?

  • Kids learn independently

    Children will learn to live for themselves without the hovering of a parent

  • Kids attain good manners

    A balance between strict and lenient creates well mannered children

  • Kids are not self-centered

    Children learn to become responsible for their actions

In depth reasons for authoritative parenting

Parents should use a style of parenting called Authoritative Parenting. Many parents raise their children how they perceive what is best for their child. Parents believe there is one way of raising a child whether it's being strict or lenient, but what many do not know that there is a balance between both styles. A child experiencing those ways of parenting would help them understand and grow and view adult life.

  • 01 Independence

    Parents should have their kids learn and experience their own feelings about pain, unhappiness, and or unpleasantness rather than having parents assist their child which could lead to being walked through life with everything being done for them. Thinking from a parent’s perspective, parents should let their child live their own and get their experience in life without being “hovered” by a parent.

  • 02 Well-Mannered

    Punishments for misbehavior are measured and consistent, not arbitrary or violent. If this does happen too much their child will never experience the bad things in life child, kids, and young adults learn this from their own point of view and experience of doing it. This will cause children to not trust their parents every time something even the smallest bit terrible happens to them.

  • 03 Altruistic

    Although some parents believe that being lenient is the best way to go, in reality giving their children everything will make their children only become self-centered and only be selfish. Parents should raise children while being strict and being lenient, a balance between the two at times in order for their child to understand and become a responsible and successful adult. The way Authoritative parenting works is “They often help their children to find appropriate outlets to solve problems. Authoritative parents encourage children to be independent but still place limits on their actions. The extensive verbal give-and-take is not refused, and parents try to be warm and nurturing toward the child.
